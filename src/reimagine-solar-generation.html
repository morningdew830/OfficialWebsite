HOW DOES SOLAR ENERGY WORK?

  1. Solar panels absorb sunlight and convert it to direct current (DC) electricity.
  2. DC electricity from the solar panels travels to inverters, where it is converted to alternating current (AC) electricity.
  3. From the inverter, AC electricity passes to the electric service panel (breaker box) where it’s routed to power your building.
  4. When your solar-energy system generates more power than your building is consuming, excess electricity is routed to the power grid. This earns credits on your bill (called net-metering).
